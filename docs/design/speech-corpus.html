Speech Corpus

A speech corpus is a collection of recordings by different people. It is used in linguistics to study speech, including accents and dialects.

The recordings will be annotated with the words and phonemes being spoken, along with the range in the recording of where they occur and binding the data to the text corpus. This allows the speech corpus to be used in the following ways:

  1. extract information about accurate transcriptions of words for use in the letter to phoneme rules and the accent modification rules;
  2. use the phoneme segments to create voices for use by the engine.

The idea is to use Public Domain or Creative Commons licensed works in the speech corpus.
